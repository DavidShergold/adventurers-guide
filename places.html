The Lands

Dornat is a land of breathtaking contrasts, where the terrain shifts from serene beauty to formidable danger with each passing mile. Its landscapes are shaped by ancient forces - both natural and mystical - that weave a rich tapestry of environments, each with its own secrets to uncover. Towering mountain ranges carve the horizon, their jagged peaks crowned with ice that never yields to the sun. Beneath them, deep valleys cradle emerald forests, where colossal trees whisper legends through rustling leaves. Some say the roots of these trees hold memories of a time before men and magic, binding the land to its history.

Across Dornat's vast expanse, biomes vary as dramatically as the tides of its shifting seas. The Northern Desolace, scorched by ancient, celestial calamities, stretch endlessly in eerie silence, while the Jungles of Gyrrahal teem with life, their tangled vines and vibrant flora defying explanation. To the north, tundra shimmer beneath spectral auroras, while the deserts of Arol-Irad hum with sombre melodies carried by relentless winds. Rivers carve lifelines through Dornat’s heart, their waters infused with alchemical mysteries. Some flow crystal-clear, while others glow faintly under moonlight, hinting at deeper enchantments.

Beyond the physical beauty of Dornat, its terrain bears the scars of legends, wars, and celestial influences. Canyons whisper with echoes of forgotten battles, and hidden caverns pulse with the energy of things best left undisturbed. Explorers and scholars alike have long attempted to map its depths, but Dornat refuses to be fully known, always shifting, always revealing new wonders to those daring enough to seek them.

Whether you tread upon its soil in search of knowledge, glory, or mere survival, one thing remains certain: no one walks Dornat unchanged.

The Empire

The Empire stretches across most of the world of Dornat and consists of more or less, the entirety of the civilised peoples. Humans, elves and dwarves make up the majority of the races within the Empire but there are others that are governed by it.

The seat of the Empire is the country of Elhorn and the city of Donst is the jewel in its crown. This city has been the home to emperor’s since the empire was founded many, many centuries ago. The empire itself was created shortly after the war against the first necromancer as a way to make certain that no war on this scale could happen again. The emperor is elected from among each country’s own rulers and they remain emperor until he dies or abdicates.

While the empire has one ruler that resides over the empire in its entirety, every country within the empire has its own ruler, king, president etc. They rule over their own country, making certain that their people abide by the empire’s rules and pulls its own weight. This last part is very important as each country is to trade equally and fairly with the other countries, ensuring that everyone gets what they need as and when they need it.

Donst, the Capital

Located in the north-western corner of Elhorn, lays the great city of Donst. This huge, mighty city is the capital of the civilised world. A centre for trade and commerce, a hub for artists and the fashion conscious, home to millions of citizens and, the throne of the empire.

Donst is often referred to as the “King’s Pie” due to its round shape. Four districts act as the slices, one for traders, one for arts and entertainment, one for scholars and the last being the fashionable noble district. Around the edge of this great wheel and forming the “crust” is the residential district and at its centre is the royal palace.

The Shada’ath Monastery

Located in the northern hills of the country of Ja’arb, the Shada’ath Monastery was founded by a runaway princess and knightly her protector and lover. The princess, who was betrothed to a prince of a neighbouring kingdom had fallen in love with her bodyguard and wishing to be together, they eloped, heading north. The young knight was granted a vision from the gods of neutrality and blessed with their knowledge. He was granted a vision and opened up a school for warriors and adventurers to attend where he could teach the arts of war and combat in all its forms, hoping that he could show people a better path of enlightenment, using their skills to preserve and defend their people.

The princess and the knight, whose names have been lost in the mists of time, were dedicated followers of the neutral gods and while there is no real deity worship enforced within the monastery, the students are encouraged to embrace the aspects and concepts of neutrality in their daily lives and philosophies. They are taught to see both sides of a situation before attempting to settle the matter in a way that is best for all parties.

The training that the students receive include all forms of warfare and combat, such as, unarmed combat, weapons of all kinds, siege engines, mounted combat, tactics, philosophy, meditation and war politics.

At the end of their training, the graduating students are given a runic brand, burnt into their forehead that will increase one of their most prominent natural abilities. For example, a fighter who is an excellent archer, might receive a rune that increases their level of eyesight enabling them to see further and clearer. Or a strong fighter could receive one that makes them more powerful physically or a nimble fighter more agile, etc.

Upon receiving this mark, they are then sent into the world to test their new skills. Many of the students spend their lives wandering the lands aiding the people, or fighting in great wars. Many of Dornat’s great heroes have graduated the Shada’ath. Some graduates return home and become a champion of their town or village, defending it from bandit, or beast attack, some become officers in the UAD. Finally, some ex students, after spending a few years honing their skills, choose to return to the Monastery to become monks and teach the next generation of students.

The Grand Melee

Every four years, the Monastery holds “The Grand Melee”. A huge tournament where warriors from around the world, come together to participate for great rewards. There are several competitions to take part in, such as archery, wrestling, jousting, fencing, but the main attraction is the Grand Melee itself. A free for all combat. This happens over several rounds. As there are normally hundreds of entrants, the first round divides them all into two teams, and these two teams clash in a huge battle. The winning team going onto the next stage.

following round is a series of preliminary heats, one on one battles, all happening at the same time across multiple battlegrounds, whittling down the participants until there are just ten. Then the finals begin. The finals happen one after another, the entrants drawing lots and fighting those they are put up against, until only one is left. This person is then crowned The Champion of the Grand Melee.

There are only a few rules in the tournament. Firstly, no enhanced mortals are allowed to participate, this includes the monks or other Shada’ath branded, people using magic to enhance themselves or the current “Chosen” of the Family of the Hero.

Also, all participants are equipped with standard weapons and armour, although they may choose the style they use.

The winners of the various competitions receive generous rewards such as gold and magical artefacts. The Grand Champion receives a special gift. They receive a “Partial-Brand” on their forehead similar to those graduating from the monastery, although not as powerful.

Shada’ath Mages

The Shada’ath Monastery also has a wing of the building inhabited by mages from Sorcerer’s Island. The students of the monastery are allowed to take classes in battle magics. The mages here also hold their own competition during the Grand Melee. A magic contest, where spell casters who have received no formal training can show off their skills. The prize for which is normally an apprenticeship under a mage back on Sorcerer’s Island.

The Holy City of Vengato

The holy city of Vengato lays on an island in the middle of Lake Venga in central Elhorn. The city was built in the year 6343BN by followers of the gods of light, who later became the first priests and eventually priest-mages. The location of the future city was shown to Valnozz Myrahel who would go on to gather other worshippers, draw up plans for the city and oversee the building before being ordained as the city’s first arch-priest.

While there had been clerics and priests operating on their own, preaching about the gods of light, this was the first time that such people had been brought together, organised and working together for the greater good instead of at odds with each other.

Within half a decade of the city’s completion, worship of the gods of light had spread across the whole of Elhorn and was becoming more popular across seas too, with many clerics from other countries coming to Vengato to be ordained as a priest of the light. Humans, elves, dwarves and even some dragons in humanoid form were enrolled.

The gods of light looked upon this magnificent city filled with grand temples, churches and cathedrals dedicated to them, and watched the men and women who had sworn servitude to them as they did good deeds in their name and brought more worshippers to their alters and smiled. They decided to reward their most dedicated followers by blessing them with the gift of miracles. They granted them the ability to perform divine magic that came from prayer to the gods as apposed to the study of natural forces as used by mages. These few could pray for protection, cleansing, healing and the most devout had been known to return life to the dead. The chosen few who could perform such feats became known as priest-mages.

Priest-mages formed the top tier of the holy orders, travelling the lands and setting up new churches in towns around Dornat and they became the foremost defence against evil attack, usually in the form of exorcisms, healing wounds or curing plague and sickness. Over time, some priest-mages decided to take a more aggressive stance against such forces, merging their holy magics with holy augmented weapons and armour. These became the first Paladins and even today such people roam the lands doing good deeds in the name of their gods. Paladins also serve as the guards of the city of Vengato defending the citizens and priests as they go about their day to day lives.

n the year 325AN, during the war against the rogue mage and cleric of the dark gods, Borovar, Vengato was completely levelled by dragon-fire while the paladins and priest-mages held off a ground attack by orcs and goblins. After Borovar was destroyed, the city was rebuilt even more glorious than before with most of it streets and buildings being constructed from pure white marble and its central cathedral coated in silver and gold.

It is in Vengato that the coronation of the new emperors of the United Countries of Dornat takes place along with royal weddings and funerals.

To the east of the city, back on the mainland is the Vengato cemetery. This cemetery is the largest on all of Dornat, measuring in at 80 acres of land. Kings, emperors, clergy, heroes and many others have been buried here since Vengato was built. Only those who have served the light may be buried here and the land is consecrated so that most creatures of darkness would be burned upon setting just one foot into the graveyard. However, this has not prevented stronger demons and devils from trying to corrupt the area or performing dark rights within the lands.

Fortunately, the cemetery is patrolled by paladins and priest-mages to keep these already rare occurencies to a minimum.

The Mausoleum

Beneath the great and holy city lays one of its darkest secrets. The speck of evil within this city of the pure. The fabled Mausoleum. Many hundreds of miles of tunnels and chambers wind their way around the underside of the city housing the corpses and remains of the most evil of people to have worked the face of Dornat.

Whenever such a person is slain by a paladin or priest-mage, their remains are brought back and laid to rest within these crypts. The walls, floors and ceiling of the Mausoleum are consecrated by the priest-mages of the city regularly and they prevent the souls of the dead from ascending to their hellish domain where they could potentially be reborn as devils. The spirits instead, are incarcerated in this morbid prison, tortured by the inherent good of the city above for all eternity

The Mausoleum is not only home to the restless dead however. Many, many years ago, after the arts of necromancy became outlawed, one practitioner of this dark magic beseeched the gods of light, offering his wicked skills to their service if he only be allowed to continue his practice. The gods of light, in their benevolence, permitted him his request. They tasked him with watching over the spirits of the damned within the Mausoleum, charged with making certain these spirits never attempt to leave or cause trouble.

In return for his endless service, the necromancer is allowed to continue his foul experiments. This man, known simply as The Attendant, whose name has been lost to all during his unnaturally long life, spends his days as jailer to the dead, harvesting their corpses to create all manner of shambling, undead abominations.

The final purpose of the Mausoleum is to test aspiring paladins. Deep within the centre of tis massive crypt lays a shrine to the gods of light. The young paladin must journey through the halls of the dead to pray at this shrine. The journey is long and hard as they must fend off the dead at every turn and constantly run the risk of getting lost within the miles of tunnels. To make matters worse, should they make it close to this shrine, the Attendant often likes to administer his own final test, pitting the young aspirant against one of his latest, wicked creations.

The Great Library

Deep in the Jungles of Lerz, there exists a tower so tall, its top is said to touch the clouds. This tower is known by the simple name, The Great Library. It is believed to have existed since the beginning of the age of mortals and its function is to record the complete history of Dornat. The current librarians are a mixture of human, elf and dwarf. However, no one knows who its original inhabitants were.

The librarians go out into the world, travelling far and wide, recording all events they see, from huge wars, to domestic celebrations. As they record, the librarians begin to use their strange and unique brand of magic…

One of the unique properties of the library is that a copy of anything that is written down anywhere in the world, from books, to shopping lists, will appear in a room at the top of the tower in a chamber they call the “Sorting Room”. One of the jobs of the librarians is to sort through everything and decide what is worth keeping. Of course, books, diaries, and scrolls are generally kept but other important things may be recorded on scraps of parchment, and as such, must be stored or re-copied into books.

As the library holds every thing ever recorded since the beginning of history, the library is also home to many manuscripts, tomes and scrolls that are incredibly dangerous. Books of dark magic and prophesy that in the wrong hands could cause irrevocable harm.

Nevertheless, the librarians have taken a very strict vow to protect all written works, regardless of their origin and as such, many of the chambers are sealed with ancient magic and in many cases, not even the lower ranked librarians are allowed access.

Access to the library by general civilians is prohibited as most of the works are ancient and as such very fragile. However, the non-prohibited works are available to all and as such the librarians will make copies of the books to be handed out. The only people who are allowed access to the library other than the librarians are mages and priests and even then, only under the strictest supervision.

The Cradle of the Elements

Deep within the desert continent of Shama’Alora, is the tallest mountain on all of Dornat. This mountain is so tall, it is said to be a ladder to the heavens themselves although none have ever scaled its rocky face. The mountain is home to the titans, huge forces of nature that guard the sacred Cradle of the Elements. This ancient artefact regulates the ebb and flow of the four elements, air, earth, fire and water, from their originating planes, to the realm of Dornat, preventing the world from having too much, or too little of each.

Sorcerer’s Island

The seat of magical studies known as wizardry is situated on a small island off the east coast of Elhorn and is known colloquially as “Sorcerer’s Island”. Although it is a modestly small island, it was chosen for the natural lines of magical energy that converge at its centre. The school was set up centuries ago after the gods removed from man the ability to use the Old Power. Mortal man, having tasted this raw power in their fingertips, did not wish to loose such power and sought mastery of a more easy to use, diluted version of energy control.

The collages themselves comprise of many magnificent and, sometimes impossible buildings. There are eight main buildings, the Grand-Master schools and the collage itself is run by the Archmage, the most powerful wizard currently alive.

School of Seeing

Students who study here are taught by the Grand-Master Seer. Here they learn the arts of seeing with the eyes of a mage instead of the eyes of a mere mortal. Using this ability, they can see things others cannot, such as the lines of magic and perhaps even the immediate future! They are also taught the art of scrying, using flames or water to see other places.

School of the Elements

These lessons are taught by the Grand-Master Elementalist. While here, the student-mage learns to have some control over the natural elements of the world, Fire, Earth, Water and Air. Conjuring these elements or bending them to the sorcerer’s whim are perhaps one of the things that mages are most known for, yet it is often considered by mages to be the hardest thing to gain full mastery over,

School of Healing

Here, the young mages are taught by the Grand-Master Healer, He or she is normally a Priest-mage from Vengato. In this school the students learn the arts of “Spoken Healing” and “Herbal Healing”. Spoken healing is the ability to heal minor words through word and thought alone. They do this by actually willing the wound to heal. This is extremely difficult though and is only usually used successfully by exceptionally skilled wizards, or those that dedicate themselves to the healing arts and become priest-mages. Herbal healing is easier for most mages to accomplish. With this they learn about the healing properties of plants etc.

School of War

Although the mages prefer to use their skills for peaceful ends, they are sometimes forced to go into combat to protect their island from evil wizards, such as liches and necromancers, or during times of war or even to simply protect themselves or companions from attack. The trainee wizards are required to spend a lot of time in this school preparing themselves for just such an eventuality.

School of Alchemy

Many of the mages potions and spell-components are comprised from various minerals. Also, much of their spell casting has its roots in scientific basis. It is in this school that the young mages learn about physics, chemicals and biology.

School of Dreams

This is the most basic of all the magical lore. All of the basest magic are formed by dreams and among the higher level mages of Sorcerer’s Island they are considered to have great power. Dreams can be used to predict future occurrences and messages can be sent via the “Dream-Scape”.

School of Power

This is the final school the mages attend before graduating. The lessons here are taught by the Archmage himself. In this school the novice mages learn the higher levels of magic and are tested several times on what they have learnt. The mages are sworn to secrecy on pain of death about what happens within this school.
